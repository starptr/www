Now that we know what [discrete derivates](/blog/discrete-derivate) are, let's see if we can derive (no pun intended) similar rules found in regular calculus.

## Product Rule

$$
\begin{aligned}
\Delta_h [fg](n)
	&= \frac{[fg](n+h) - [fg](n)}{h} \\
	&= \frac{f(n+h)g(n+h) - f(n)g(n)}{h} \\
	&= \frac{f(n+h)g(n+h) - f(n+h)g(n) + f(n+h)g(n) - f(n)g(n)}{h} \\
	&= \frac{f(n+h)(g(n+h) - g(n)) + g(n)(f(n+h)-f(n))}{h} \\
	&= f(n+h) \frac{g(n+h) - g(n)}{h} + g(n) \frac{f(n+h) - f(n)}{h} \\
	&= f(n+h) \Delta_h g(n) + g(n) \Delta_h f(n) \\
\end{aligned}
$$

## Quotient Rule

$$
\begin{aligned}
\Delta_h \left[\frac{f}{g}\right](n)
	&= \frac{\left[\frac{f}{g}\right](n+h) - \left[\frac{f}{g}\right](n)}{h} \\
	&= \frac{\frac{f(n+h)}{g(n+h)} - \frac{f(n)}{g(n)}}{h} \\
	&= \frac{1}{h} \frac{f(n+h)g(n) - f(n)g(n+h)}{g(n+h)g(n)} \\
	&= \frac{1}{g(n+h)g(n)} \frac{f(n+h)g(n) - f(n)g(n) + f(n)g(n) - f(n)g(n+h)}{h} \\
	&= \frac{1}{g(n+h)g(n)} \frac{g(n)(f(n+h) - f(n)) + f(n)(g(n) - g(n+h))}{h} \\
	&= \frac{1}{g(n+h)g(n)} \left(g(n) \frac{f(n+h) - f(n)}{h} - f(n) \frac{g(n+h) - g(n)}{h}\right) \\
	&= \frac{g(n) \Delta_h f(n) - f(n) \Delta_h g(n)}{g(n+h)g(n)} \\
\end{aligned}
$$
